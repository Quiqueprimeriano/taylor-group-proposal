01 — The Opportunity

95 Years of Growth. One Moment of Strategic Advantage.

Taylor Group has grown organically for 95 years into an enterprise-scale operation — $85M revenue, 321 people, spanning 40+ countries. But this growth has been powered by startup-era processes. That's not a criticism; it's a testament to the caliber of the people. The question isn't whether Taylor has succeeded — it's what becomes possible when you add a digital foundation to that talent.

The experiential marketing industry is at an inflection point. The market has reached $15.8B, with 74% of brands increasing event budgets. But the landscape is shifting radically: lead times that were 8 weeks are now 2 weeks. AI-powered competitors are entering the space. The companies that thrive won't be the ones with the most people — they'll be the ones with the smartest operations.

The tools are already in place. ClickUp, Gemini, Microsoft 365 — Taylor has invested in the technology. But without a unified strategy, these tools sit dormant. The investment has been made; the return hasn't been realized.

There is no CTO. No unified technology roadmap. No connective tissue between tools, teams, and offices. This isn't unusual for companies that grew through acquisition and organic expansion — but it means every department has optimized in isolation.

Only 16% of digital transformations fully succeed (McKinsey). That means doing this right matters more than doing it fast. The approach matters. The sequencing matters. The culture matters.

The Cost of Waiting

Urgency

Every quarter without a unified digital strategy:

  • Redundant asset fabrication continues unchecked — estimated $125K–$300K per quarter
  • Sales↔production misalignment compounds with growth — each new market Taylor enters multiplies the coordination gap
  • AI-powered competitors are already operationalizing — Freeman, GPJ, and Jack Morton have publicly announced AI and automation programs
  • The ClickUp, Gemini, and M365 investments depreciate without activation — Taylor is paying for capability it isn't using
  • The talent gap widens — the longer adoption is delayed, the harder the eventual change

This is not a 2027 problem with a 2026 option. The operational cost is being paid today. The question is how long Taylor continues to absorb it.

The “Digital Backbone” is what transforms Taylor from a collection of talented teams into one connected, AI-amplified operation. Not new software — new connective tissue.

CREATE — Strategy & Creative

The backbone helps teams think faster, think smarter, and create with more context.

In the Create layer, the backbone enables teams to think with more context, move faster, and develop ideas that are better connected to business reality. Today, ideation depends almost entirely on the individuals in the room — their memory, their relationships, their experience. The backbone changes that equation.

Strategic Intelligence

The backbone gives strategy and creative teams access to broader and better inputs at the start of the process:

  • Client history and past program knowledge
  • Competitor activity and experiential benchmarks
  • Market trends and directional signals
  • Category patterns and emerging formats
  • Prior campaign learnings and results

This shifts ideation away from isolated inspiration and toward better-informed creative thinking.

Creative Memory

Without a backbone, ideas live in people, decks, folders, and scattered files. With a backbone, the organization gains:

  • Searchable archives of concepts, builds, and proposals
  • Visibility into previous ideas developed for similar clients or sectors
  • A reusable body of intellectual and creative work

This reduces reinvention and strengthens continuity across teams.

Proposal Acceleration

A major value unlock in Create is speed. The backbone helps teams respond to opportunities faster, assemble proposals using past materials and pricing logic, build early concepts with stronger grounding in what has already been done, and shorten the time between brief and first strategic output. The business can think faster, propose faster, and generate more ideas.

Better Forecasting During Ideation

The strongest ideas are not only exciting — they are also feasible. The backbone brings early visibility into approximate production complexity, likely resource intensity, delivery implications, and whether similar components or assets already exist. This creates a better link between creative ambition and operational reality.

AI-Assisted Ideation

AI matters here, but as an accelerator inside a broader technology foundation. Potential value includes concept expansion, reference generation, draft narrative support, alternative routes for creative exploration, and faster synthesis of market and client inputs. The point is not automated creativity, but augmented creative velocity.

CREATE becomes: more informed, faster, more reusable, more grounded in reality, and more capable of generating quality ideas at speed.

BeforeAfter
Ideas start from scratch, proposals assembled manually, institutional knowledge lives in people's heads Institutional memory searchable, proposals accelerated, creative ambition grounded in operational reality

BUILD — Digital Experiences & Fabrication

The backbone helps great ideas become buildable, scalable, and operationally viable.

In the Build layer, the backbone connects concept to production. This is where the business translates ideas into things that can actually be made, configured, integrated, shipped, and deployed. It's Taylor's operational core — and it's where the biggest efficiency gaps live.

Design-to-Build Continuity

One of the biggest friction points in complex project businesses is the handoff between creative intent and production reality. The backbone improves this by enabling:

  • Continuity between design outputs and production inputs
  • Shared documentation across creative, digital, and fabrication teams
  • Clearer specifications and less interpretation loss
  • Stronger alignment between what is imagined and what is actually buildable

This reduces downstream confusion and rework.

Asset Intelligence

The backbone creates visibility into what the business already owns, has built, or can reuse:

  • Reusable scenic elements and prior fabrication components
  • Historic build references and material precedents
  • Digital assets connected to physical experiences

Instead of starting from zero, teams can build from memory.

Materials & Component Visibility

The backbone creates a stronger picture of what is needed to build and what is already available — supporting material planning, component tracking, procurement coordination, and supplier-related visibility. This reduces unnecessary friction and helps control costs and timing.

Capacity & Production Forecasting

A major theme from discovery was the need to better align sales growth with production capacity. The backbone makes it easier to understand current production load, team capacity, likely constraints, timing conflicts across projects, and build implications of incoming pipeline. This is where the business starts moving from reactive production to planned production.

Digital–Physical Integration

Taylor is unusual because Build includes both digital experiences and physical fabrication. The backbone creates alignment across physical environments, interactive experiences, content production, platform integration, and on-site digital behavior. This is a major strategic advantage if connected well.

Cost Intelligence

The backbone improves the ability to estimate and manage cost before problems surface — benchmark against past builds, anticipate cost drivers, understand design decisions with production implications, and price work with better historical reference. Build becomes not just faster, but more commercially informed.

BUILD becomes: more coordinated, more predictable, more reusable, more cost-aware, and more connected across digital and physical work.

BeforeAfter
Sales sells blind, assets rebuilt because nobody knows they exist, each office runs its own way Design-to-build continuity, searchable asset inventory, planned production, cost intelligence from history

EXECUTE — Program Management & Measurement

The backbone turns execution from something people discover too late into something they can manage actively.

In the Execute layer, the backbone enables orchestration. This is where complexity peaks, because projects move from internal planning into real-world delivery. It's also where Taylor's leadership spends the most time today — often manually.

Project Orchestration

Execution depends on multiple teams, timelines, vendors, dependencies, and deliverables moving together. The backbone helps create:

  • Centralized project visibility and shared timelines
  • Better coordination across departments
  • Clearer dependencies and ownership
  • Stronger alignment between planning and delivery

This is the shift from fragmented execution to connected execution.

Resource Allocation

Execution quality depends on whether the right people and capabilities are available at the right moment. The backbone supports better crew planning, specialist allocation, visibility into team utilization, and cross-project resource balancing. This reduces overload, blind spots, and fire-fighting.

Logistics & Deployment Visibility

In experiential work, execution involves physical movement, deployment, timing, setup, breakdown, and on-site coordination. The backbone improves logistics tracking, deployment readiness, handoff from fabrication to site, installation sequencing, and clarity around what is arriving where and when. This reduces execution risk.

Real-Time Execution Visibility

One of the highest-value outcomes of a backbone is making delivery visible while it is happening. Project status can be monitored more clearly, emerging issues can be seen earlier, leadership can intervene sooner, and decisions can be made with better information. When execution is visible in a system, leadership doesn't need to be CC'd on every email to stay informed.

Measurement & Learning

Taylor's Execute layer includes Measurement — execution does not end at delivery. The backbone captures event outcomes, attendee engagement, lead conversion data, performance patterns across formats, and post-event learnings. This closes the loop between delivery and future strategy.

Continuous Improvement

Once execution data is captured and connected, the business gains something bigger than reporting: a learning system. Each project informs the next. Recurring issues become visible. High-performing formats become identifiable. The company becomes better over time, not just busier.

EXECUTE becomes: more visible, more coordinated, more predictable, more measurable, and more capable of learning over time.

BeforeAfter
Status via email chains, reactive fire-fighting, post-event learnings lost, no unified view Real-time visibility, connected execution, organizational learning compounds over time
