01 — McKinsey

The 4Ds Framework + 10 Success Factors

What

  • 4 phases: Discover, Design, Deliver, De-risk
  • 6 assessment dimensions: Strategy, Process, Organization & Talent, Technology & Data, Risk & Compliance, Partnerships & Ecosystem
  • 10 success factors that differentiate the 16% of transformations that fully succeed

How

  • Start with lighthouse projects — quick wins that demonstrate value and self-fund the transformation
  • Secure senior leadership buy-in first, then cascade through the organization
  • Design a new operating model, not just new tools
  • Adopt agile ways of working across the transformation program itself
  • Use the 6 dimensions as a diagnostic lens: assess each independently, then map interdependencies

Why It Works

  • Sequences initiatives for momentum — early wins build confidence and budget
  • The 10 success factors are empirically derived from analyzing hundreds of transformations
  • Balances ambition with pragmatism through the lighthouse approach
Key Statistic

Only 16% of digital transformations fully succeed. McKinsey's research shows that companies addressing all 10 success factors are 2.5x more likely to achieve transformation goals. 46% of executives say cultural change is the single biggest challenge.

Relevance to Taylor Group

  • Taylor's transformation should start with lighthouse projects (asset tracking, email automation) that demonstrate value before scaling
  • The culture dimension is critical — 95 years of institutional culture requires a respect-first approach
  • The 6-dimension framework maps directly to our 5-pillar approach
02 — Deloitte

The Capability Hexagon

What

  • 6 dimensions per business capability: Mission, Insights, Processes, Technology, Talent, Governance
  • Each capability is assessed across all 6 dimensions to identify gaps
  • 5 maturity levels: Ad-hoc → Repeatable → Defined → Managed → Optimized
  • The hexagon is a diagnostic and design tool — it visualizes where capabilities are strong and where they're weak

How

  • Map each business capability (e.g., "Asset Management," "Sales Operations") across all 6 dimensions
  • Identify dimension gaps: having great Technology but weak Processes or Governance means the tech won't be adopted
  • Don't implement technology without first closing gaps in Talent, Process, and Governance
  • Use the 5 maturity levels to set realistic targets — you don't go from Ad-hoc to Optimized in one phase
  • Design interventions that address the weakest dimension first

Why It Works

  • Prevents the classic failure mode: "We bought the tool but nobody uses it"
  • Forces holistic thinking — you can't just address technology in isolation
  • The hexagon visualization makes gaps obvious and communicable to stakeholders
Key Insight

Deloitte's central thesis: "Successful transformation requires equal focus on business design AND digital solution design." Technology without process redesign is shelfware. Process redesign without talent development is unsustainable.

Relevance to Taylor Group

  • Taylor's ClickUp Brain situation is a textbook Deloitte case: Technology dimension is present, but Talent (training), Process (workflows), and Governance (policies) dimensions are at Level 1
  • The hexagon approach validates our decision to start with People & Culture before Tech Integrations
  • Asset management needs assessment across all 6 dimensions, not just a technology solution
03 — BCG

Digital Acceleration Index (DAI)

What

  • 4 building blocks: Digital Strategy, Digitizing the Core, New Digital Growth, Key Enablers
  • Quantitative maturity scoring across 36 indicators
  • Benchmarking against industry peers and digital leaders
  • Focus on measuring digital maturity, not just planning it

How

  • Start with quick wins that self-fund the transformation — BCG calls these "no-regret moves"
  • Move across all 6 dimensions in parallel (don't sequence purely linearly)
  • Use the DAI score to benchmark progress quarterly
  • Focus on "Digitizing the Core" first — this is where the ROI is most immediate and measurable
  • Build the "Key Enablers" (data, talent, governance) simultaneously

Why It Works

  • Data-driven assessment removes opinion and politics from prioritization
  • Quick wins build both momentum and budget — transformation becomes self-funding
  • The DAI provides an objective benchmark that leadership can track over time
  • Parallel execution across dimensions prevents bottlenecks
Key Approach

BCG's "no-regret moves" philosophy: identify initiatives that deliver value regardless of which strategic direction the company ultimately pursues. For Taylor, this means email automation, asset cataloging, and ClickUp optimization are no-regret moves — they deliver value no matter what.

Relevance to Taylor Group

  • The DAI scoring approach could be adapted to create Taylor's own transformation scorecard
  • "Digitizing the Core" maps to our Processes and Tech Integrations pillars
  • BCG's parallel execution philosophy validates addressing People & Culture alongside Tech, not sequentially
04 — Accenture

Total Enterprise Reinvention

What

  • Operating model redesign with data and technology as the core driver
  • Customer-centric multidisciplinary teams + enterprise platform teams
  • Not a digital overlay on an existing model — a fundamental redesign of how the business operates
  • The most ambitious framework — designed for companies ready for radical change

How

  • Redesign the operating model around data — every decision, process, and interaction is data-informed
  • Create multidisciplinary teams organized around customer outcomes, not functional silos
  • Build enterprise platform teams that maintain shared infrastructure and tools
  • Replace legacy operating model entirely, not incrementally

Why It Works

  • Most ambitious and comprehensive approach — addresses root causes, not symptoms
  • Data-centricity ensures decisions are evidence-based from the start
  • Team structure redesign breaks down silos permanently, not temporarily
Key Distinction

Accenture's approach differs fundamentally from others: it doesn't optimize the existing model — it replaces it. This is the right framework for companies where the current operating model is a structural barrier to growth. It's the most disruptive but also the most transformative.

Relevance to Taylor Group

  • Taylor isn't ready for total enterprise reinvention — and that's fine. The current priority is connecting and optimizing what exists
  • However, the data-centricity principle is directly applicable: Taylor's transformation should be building toward data-informed decision-making at every level
  • The multidisciplinary team concept is relevant for the DISCOVERY phase — assembling cross-functional stakeholders
05 — Industry Standard

The Classic Framework: People, Process, Technology, Data

What

  • The original 4-pillar transformation framework, increasingly extended to 5 pillars with Governance/Compliance
  • Universal across industries: manufacturing, services, technology, healthcare
  • Simple enough to communicate to any stakeholder, robust enough to structure a multi-year program
  • The foundation that every consulting framework builds upon

How

  • Assess each pillar independently — current state, desired state, gap analysis
  • Map interdependencies between pillars: Technology changes require Process changes, which require People changes
  • Address the weakest pillar first — it's the bottleneck that limits all others
  • Add Governance as the fifth pillar when AI, data privacy, or regulatory concerns are present

Why It Works

  • Simplicity is its strength — executives, managers, and front-line employees all understand it
  • Forces acknowledgment that technology alone doesn't transform anything
  • The interdependency mapping reveals hidden blockers and dependencies

Case Studies

Company Transformation
AT&T Invested $1B in reskilling 280,000 employees for digital roles. Technology was available — the bottleneck was People. The reskilling program was the transformation.
Domino's Pizza 65% of US sales now come from digital channels. The transformation wasn't about building an app — it was about redesigning processes around digital-first customer experience.
DBS Bank Named "World's Best Digital Bank" — achieved through a 5-year transformation focused equally on culture change, process redesign, and technology modernization.
Key Principle

Every consulting framework — McKinsey, Deloitte, BCG, Accenture — is fundamentally an elaboration of People, Process, Technology, Data. The value of the more sophisticated frameworks lies in the diagnostic tools and sequencing methodologies they provide.

Relevance to Taylor Group

  • TransformAZ's 5-pillar framework is a deliberate extension of this classic model
  • The addition of Compliance as a distinct pillar (rather than folding it into Governance) reflects Taylor's specific client requirements (pharma, healthcare, financial services)
  • The framework's simplicity makes it communicable to Taylor's leadership without consulting jargon
