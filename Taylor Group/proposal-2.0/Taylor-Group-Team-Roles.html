01 — Overview

Three Roles. One Backbone.

The Taylor Group transformation requires a lean, high-impact team of three consultants, each with a distinct area of ownership. The team structure follows industry best practices (McKinsey, Deloitte, BCG) adapted for mid-market engagements where every person wears multiple hats and there is no room for role ambiguity.

The engagement spans 5 phases, 138 tasks, and approximately 15 months — from pre-engagement scoping through advisory retainer. The three roles work in parallel, intersecting at defined touchpoints and governed by a clear RACI matrix.

Fermín

Engagement Lead

Client relationship, strategic direction, stakeholder alignment, executive presentations, governance. The single point of accountability for the engagement.

Agustín

Process & Implementation Consultant

Process mapping, stakeholder interviews, SOP design, workflow optimization, implementation execution, adoption tracking. The bridge between strategy and execution.

[Tech Lead]

Technology & Solutions Architect

ClickUp architecture, Gemini deployment, M365 integration, automation engine, dashboards, data infrastructure. The builder who turns designs into systems.

Design principle: Fermín owns the what and the why. Agustín owns the how (processes and people). Tech Lead owns the how (systems and tools). All three converge on the when (delivery).

Fermín — Engagement Lead

Principal

Strategic direction, client management, executive alignment, quality assurance

Core Responsibilities

  • Client relationship owner. Single point of contact for Dean, Mike, and the executive team. Manages expectations, escalates risks, steers toward consensus.
  • Strategic direction. Sets the engagement hypothesis (McKinsey approach), defines priorities, and makes scope decisions. Final authority on what gets done and in what order.
  • Executive alignment. Facilitates steering committee sessions, presents findings and recommendations, and secures buy-in at every phase gate.
  • Quality assurance. Reviews every deliverable before it reaches the client. Ensures narrative coherence across process, technology, and people workstreams.
  • Governance & scope. Manages the RACI matrix, change requests, and scope boundaries. Protects the team from scope creep while maintaining client satisfaction.
  • Team coordination. Runs internal syncs, unblocks team members, and manages resource allocation across phases.

Agustín — Process & Implementation Consultant

Operations

Process mapping, stakeholder interviews, SOP design, implementation execution, adoption

Core Responsibilities

  • Stakeholder interviews. Conducts 3-4 interviews per day during Discovery using semi-structured guides. Interviews functional teams (Ops, Creative, Sales, IT, Warehouse) and cross-office staff (Boston, Cleveland remotely). Documents both the formal process (“how it should work”) and the actual process (“how it really works”).
  • Process mapping. Builds current-state and future-state process maps using SIPOC, swim lane diagrams, and value stream mapping. Maps workflows across the Create→Build→Execute cycle. Identifies bottlenecks, redundancies, manual workarounds, and automation opportunities.
  • SOP design & modernization. Documents existing SOPs for priority workflows, then designs future-state SOPs with automation touchpoints. Ensures SOPs are written for actual users, not filing cabinets.
  • Gap analysis. Quantifies waste: time lost, error rates, rework percentages, handoff delays. Maps gaps to the 5 transformation dimensions (Deloitte Hexagon approach).
  • Adoption & change support. Recruits and manages internal champions (3-5 per department). Designs segmented training paths. Conducts bi-weekly adoption pulse checks. Bridges the gap between what’s designed and what people actually do.
  • Implementation execution. Drives process optimization workstream during Phase 3: cross-office standardization, sales-production alignment, estimation frameworks. Works with Tech Lead to ensure automations match redesigned processes.

Interview & Research Methodology

  • Preparation: Reviews existing documentation (SOPs, org charts, job descriptions) before every interview. Prepares semi-structured discussion guides organized by functional area and mapped to transformation dimensions.
  • Technique: Opens with rapport-building, then uses directed questions (“Walk me through exactly what happens when a new client order comes in”) and open-ended probes (“What would you change about how your team communicates with operations?”). Probes for quantitative data: cycle times, error rates, handoff delays.
  • Frameworks: SIPOC (high-level), Current-State Process Maps (detailed), Value Stream Mapping (waste identification), Swim Lane Diagrams (cross-functional responsibility).
  • Tools: Miro/Lucidchart for process maps, ClickUp for task tracking, structured interview templates, observation checklists for gemba walks.
  • Output: Each interview produces a structured summary with: key quotes, process observations, pain points identified, automation opportunities flagged, dimension mapping.

[Tech Lead] — Technology & Solutions Architect

Technology

ClickUp architecture, Gemini/AI deployment, M365 integration, automations, dashboards, data infrastructure

Core Responsibilities

  • Technology audit & assessment. Inventories all current tools, licenses, integrations, and actual usage patterns. Identifies tool sprawl, underutilization, and integration gaps. Evaluates ClickUp Brain status, Gemini deployment, M365 configuration.
  • Architecture design. Designs the overall ClickUp workspace structure (Spaces, Folders, Lists, status flows, custom fields). Architects integrations between ClickUp, Google Workspace, M365, and automation middleware. Produces the technology architecture document.
  • Automation engine. Builds and tests all workflow automations: approval chains, status-triggered notifications, auto-assignment rules, recurring task generation. Targets 10-15 priority automations across the engagement.
  • AI deployment. Configures and deploys Gemini for email triage (executive team), meeting summarization, and content drafting. Activates ClickUp Brain across priority workspaces. Defines AI tool configurations and usage patterns.
  • Dashboard & data infrastructure. Designs and builds executive dashboards (capacity visibility, project profitability, unified operational view). Creates the asset cataloging system. Deploys data tracking frameworks.
  • Training & documentation. Conducts hands-on tech training sessions. Creates video walkthroughs and quick-reference guides. Provides “office hours” support during post-launch weeks. Documents all configurations and architecture decisions.

Technical Skill Requirements

  • ClickUp expert: Workspace architecture, custom fields, automations, Brain/AI features, API integrations, template design
  • Google Workspace / Gemini: Admin configuration, Gemini AI features, Gmail rules and routing, Calendar integration, Drive organization
  • Microsoft 365: SharePoint/Teams administration, Power Automate (for M365-side automations), Excel/Power BI for dashboards
  • Integration tools: Make (Integromat), Zapier, or n8n for cross-platform automation. API literacy for custom integrations.
  • Data: Dashboard design (ClickUp dashboards, Google Sheets, Power BI), data modeling, basic SQL for data extraction if needed
