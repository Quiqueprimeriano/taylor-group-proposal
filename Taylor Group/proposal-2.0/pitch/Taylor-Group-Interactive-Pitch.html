Taylor Group has grown organically for 95 years into an enterprise-scale operation — $85M revenue, 321 people, spanning 40+ countries. But this growth has been powered by startup-era processes. That's not a criticism; it's a testament to the caliber of the people. The question isn't whether Taylor has succeeded — it's what becomes possible when you add a digital foundation to that talent.

The experiential marketing industry is at an inflection point. Global spend hit a record $128B in 2024, with 74% of Fortune 1000 marketers increasing event budgets. But the landscape is shifting: lead times that were 8 weeks are now 2 weeks. AI-powered competitors are entering the space. The companies that thrive won't be the ones with the most people — they'll be the ones with the smartest operations.

The tools are already in place. ClickUp, Gemini, Microsoft 365 — Taylor has invested in the technology. But without a unified strategy, these tools sit dormant. The investment has been made; the return hasn't been realized.

03 — The Digital Backbone

One Connected Operation

Think of the Digital Backbone as the nervous system of Taylor Group's operation. It connects every part of Taylor's business: sales, production, assets, communication, and decisions. Information flows seamlessly. Production capacity is visible to sales in real time. Assets are tracked and reusable. AI amplifies human talent rather than replacing it.

Critically, the Digital Backbone is built on what Taylor already owns. This isn't about buying new software. It's about activating what's there, connecting what's siloed, and adding the intelligence layer that turns tools into a system.

Five Dimensions Across Every Phase

People & Culture

Dimension

Segmented adoption paths for executives, operations, and creative teams. Internal champions program (3-5 per department). Skills development roadmap by role. Culture-first change management that respects 95 years of institutional identity.

“Technology changes fast. Culture changes slow. We start with culture.”

Processes

Dimension

SOP modernization. Sales-to-production alignment with real-time capacity visibility. Asset management lifecycle. Cross-office standardization across Toronto, Boston, and Cleveland.

“Before automating a process, make sure it's the right process.”

Tech Integrations

Dimension

ClickUp architecture optimization + Brain activation. Gemini integration for email triage, meeting notes, and drafts. Automation engine (10-15 priority workflows). Global Core ↔ Operations tool alignment.

“The best technology strategy starts with what you already own.”

Data

Dimension

Asset inventory intelligence. Production capacity data. Project profitability tracking. Executive dashboards. Performance feedback loops that close the Create→Build→Execute cycle.

“You can't optimize what you can't measure.”

Compliance

Dimension

AI governance policy. Data handling standards for client IP, internal IP, and vendor data. IP protection strategy. Regulatory readiness for pharma and healthcare clients.

“Innovation without guardrails is a liability.”

Five dimensions. Three phases. One backbone. The Digital Backbone makes Taylor more informed in Create, more coordinated in Build, and more governed in Execute.

Digital Backbone

The backbone helps teams think faster, think smarter, and create with more context.

In the Create layer, ideation depends almost entirely on the individuals in the room — their memory, their relationships, their experience. The backbone changes that equation.

Strategic Intelligence

Client history, competitor activity, market trends, and prior campaign learnings available at the start of every brief. Ideation shifts from isolated inspiration to informed creative thinking.

Creative Memory

Searchable archives of concepts, builds, and proposals. Visibility into previous ideas for similar clients or sectors. A reusable body of intellectual and creative work.

Proposal Acceleration

Respond to opportunities faster. Assemble proposals using past materials and pricing logic. Shorten the time between brief and first strategic output.

Better Forecasting

Early visibility into production complexity, resource intensity, delivery implications, and whether similar components already exist. Creative ambition meets operational reality.

AI-Assisted Ideation

Concept expansion, reference generation, draft narrative support, and faster synthesis of market inputs. Not automated creativity — augmented creative velocity.

CREATE becomes: more informed, faster, more reusable, more grounded in reality, and more capable of generating quality ideas at speed.

BeforeAfter
Ideas start from scratch, proposals assembled manually, institutional knowledge lives in people's headsInstitutional memory searchable, proposals accelerated, creative ambition grounded in operational reality

The backbone helps great ideas become buildable, scalable, and operationally viable.

In the Build layer, the backbone connects concept to production. This is Taylor's operational core — and it's where the biggest efficiency gaps live.

Design-to-Build Continuity

Continuity between design outputs and production inputs. Shared documentation across creative, digital, and fabrication teams. Less interpretation loss.

Asset Intelligence

Visibility into what the business already owns, has built, or can reuse. Reusable scenic elements, historic build references, digital assets connected to physical experiences.

Materials & Components

Stronger picture of what is needed vs. what is available. Material planning, component tracking, procurement coordination, and supplier visibility.

Capacity Forecasting

Production load, team capacity, timing conflicts, and build implications of incoming pipeline. The shift from reactive to planned production.

Digital–Physical Integration

Alignment across physical environments, interactive experiences, content production, platform integration, and on-site digital behavior.

Cost Intelligence

Benchmark against past builds, anticipate cost drivers, price work with better historical reference. Build becomes not just faster, but more commercially informed.

BUILD becomes: more coordinated, more predictable, more reusable, more cost-aware, and more connected across digital and physical work.

BeforeAfter
Sales sells blind, assets rebuilt because nobody knows they exist, each office runs its own wayDesign-to-build continuity, searchable asset inventory, planned production, cost intelligence from history

The backbone turns execution from something people discover too late into something they can manage actively.

In the Execute layer, complexity peaks. Projects move from internal planning into real-world delivery. It's where Taylor's leadership spends the most time today — often manually.

Project Orchestration

Centralized project visibility, shared timelines, clearer dependencies and ownership. The shift from fragmented execution to connected execution.

Resource Allocation

Better crew planning, specialist allocation, visibility into team utilization, and cross-project resource balancing. Fewer blind spots and less fire-fighting.

Logistics & Deployment

Logistics tracking, deployment readiness, installation sequencing, and clarity around what is arriving where and when. Reduced execution risk.

Real-Time Visibility

Project status monitored clearly, emerging issues seen earlier, leadership intervenes sooner. No need to be CC'd on every email to stay informed.

Measurement & Learning

Event outcomes, attendee engagement, lead conversion, performance patterns. The loop between delivery and future strategy — closed.

Continuous Improvement

Each project informs the next. Recurring issues become visible. High-performing formats become identifiable. The company becomes better over time, not just busier.

EXECUTE becomes: more visible, more coordinated, more predictable, more measurable, and more capable of learning over time.

BeforeAfter
