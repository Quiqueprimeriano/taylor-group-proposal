TransformAZ Value Proposition for Taylor Group

Why does Taylor need TransformAZ?

Taylor Group is an $85M company with 321 employees that has grown organically over 95 years. They have the scale of an enterprise company but operate with the processes of a company that grew without a formal technology structure. They have no CTO. Their tech stack was adopted reactively (ClickUp because they needed PM, Gemini because Google, M365 because finance). There is no unified technology strategy.

The timing is critical:

  • The B2B trade show industry reached $15.8 billion in 2024 — it is at historic highs
  • 74% of Fortune 1000 marketers plan to increase event budgets
  • But lead times compressed from 8 to 2 weeks — operational speed is now a competitive differentiator
  • Only 25% of organizations achieve the expected ROI from AI investments — implementing poorly is worse than not implementing at all
  • 70% of change programs fail — change management is not optional

Value Proposition Statement

TransformAZ helps Taylor Group turn 95 years of operational knowledge into a competitive technological advantage — activating the tools they already have, optimizing the processes that already work, and preparing the team for a future where speed and operational intelligence define who wins the enterprise contracts.

Section 04

Findings — What we discovered in the meetings

Finding 1: Asset Management is money being thrown away

Direct evidence (Dean, Call 2):

“We build a lot of custom things, and then we store them for clients. Sometimes they own them, sometimes they don’t, and we don’t do a great job of tracking them, because everything has to be dismantled to ship. It’s like Ikea then assembled on site and then disassembled back into the IKEA boxes, and then it just stays. And you can’t make sense of it. We ended up rebuilding a lot of things.”

— Dean Marks, Call 2

Industry context:

  • The B2B trade show market is $15.8B. Custom-fabricated exhibits represent a significant fraction of the cost per event.
  • Companies like Genesis Exhibits already offer Asset Management Systems (AssetPRO) with: real-time tracking, usage history, inventory coordinates, lifecycle management.
  • Solutions include: barcodes, RFID tags, centralized ERPs, specialized platforms like ExhibitForce and ExhibitDay.
  • ROI of well-implemented asset tracking: cost reduction through reuse, elimination of emergency shipments, consistent brand protection, sustainability compliance.
Potential quantification

If Taylor fabricates ~50-100 custom projects/year with an average fabrication cost of $50K-150K, and they rebuild 15-20% of what they could have reused:

Annual cost of not tracking assets: $375K–$3M (wide range, Phase 1 quantifies it precisely)

Even the conservative end ($375K) justifies the entire program investment.

Finding 2: The Sales↔Production disconnect is the main bottleneck

Direct evidence (Dean, Call 2):

“The biggest opportunity is good structural alignment between sales growth and production capacity. Somebody would sell something. Here’s a picture, send it out to the shop. Yeah, we can build it. And we hope for the best.”

— Dean Marks, Call 2

What this means:

  • Sales sells without visibility into actual production capacity
  • Production receives projects without sufficient context on what was sold
  • The “people on site are the last line of defense to fix anything we didn’t do”
  • This worked with 8-week lead times. With 2 weeks, every mistake costs exponentially more.
Potential quantification

With 2-week lead times, every misalignment = overtime hours, rush shipments, show floor errors.

If 5-10% of projects have alignment issues, and the resolution cost is $5K-15K per incident:

Estimated annual impact: $250K–$1.5M

Finding 3: Executive time is consumed by routing, not decision-making

Direct evidence (Mike, Call 1):

“Say I’m getting 400 emails, 500 emails a day. How do I take the 70% of emails that I get CC’d on? How do I make those as read emails? How do we filter them? If we can make a 10% improvement in efficiencies, that’s a lot on a business our size.”

— Mike, Call 1

What this means:

  • Mike (EVP) and Frank (VP Healthcare) are operating as “human information routers”
  • 70% of emails are CC → information they need to see but not act on
  • The executives of an $85M company are spending their most expensive time on the most automatable task
  • ClickUp Brain and Gemini could solve this, but nobody knows how to configure them
Quantification

If 3 executives lose 2 hrs/day on non-strategic email.

Estimated executive time value: $150-200/hr (opportunity cost).

Annual cost: $225K–$300K

Target reduction of 40%: $90K–$120K recovered/year

Finding 4: ClickUp Brain is paid for and deactivated

Direct evidence (Mike, Call 1):

“We have ClickUp Brain, which leverages Chat GPT and Claude and Gemini. We have the ability through ClickUp, they can build some agents for us. We’re kind of stuck about how do we use those agents? What do we do with them? We don’t know enough about it to even understand it.”

— Mike, Call 1

What this means:

  • They have an AI layer integrated into their main PM tool
  • They are paying for it (ClickUp Brain is a $5-7/user/month add-on)
  • With 321 employees, if only 100 have ClickUp with Brain: $6K-8.4K/year paid with no return
  • But the real cost is not the license — it is the lost opportunity to automate status queries, weekly summaries, client update drafts, and answers to recurring questions

Finding 5: The generational gap is real but not the threat they think

Direct evidence (Dean, Call 2):

“You can take a designer, a guy that designs exhibits, maybe from Colombia with a very thick accent, can’t pitch. You’re going to have these people who have talents, who used to start from scratch that are now using AI tools.”

— Dean Marks, Call 2

Direct evidence (Mike, Call 1):

“Some of the folks that are above me, like our CFO, they’re pushing 60, they’re stuck in their ways.”

— Mike, Call 1

What this means:

  • There are two populations with different needs: early adopters (younger, already using AI individually) and resisters (senior, “stuck in their ways”)
  • The risk is NOT that senior people won’t adopt — it is that younger employees adopt without governance and senior people are left without tools that make their work easier
  • Dean was explicit: “Even if people are affected by this, we will make sure that we take good care of people. Because if people are looking over their shoulder and worried about how — we just know this is a very sensitive thing.”
  • The transformation needs to segment the audience and design different adoption paths

Finding 6: No CTO — a strategic gap that we fill

Direct evidence (Dean, Call 2):

“We do not have a CTO, but we’ve, you know, despite that, have gotten to the point where we’re pretty large.”

— Dean Marks, Call 2

What this means:

  • An $85M company with 321 employees and operations in 5 cities does not have a person who owns the technology strategy
  • Tech decisions are made reactively (“we looked at ClickUp”, “we added Chat”)
  • There is no technology roadmap, no tool governance, no cross-office standards
  • TransformAZ can position itself as fractional CTO/advisor — not just during the program, but as a permanent retainer
Key Opportunity

The absence of a CTO in an $85M company creates a natural strategic gap. TransformAZ can fill that role during the program and transition to a permanent advisory retainer — $54K/year in recurring revenue with 81-85% margins.

Finding 7: Dassault Systèmes already showed them the future

Direct evidence (Dean, Call 2):

“Through Intel, we work very closely with Dassault Systèmes... they’re doing things like scanning the manufacturing facility, 3D scanning it, looking at from the time the work order comes in till the time the final invoice goes out. And how do we optimize that? When the project opens and it’s speccing aluminum, it might tell you that aluminum prices may go up in six months, so we should buy now.”

— Dean Marks, Call 2

What this means:

  • Dean already has a vision of what is possible (he saw it at a client)
  • He knows that SolidWorks/Dassault are building a “3D universe” for end-to-end optimization
  • This becomes the aspirational benchmark for the AI Vision document
  • We can reference this example as “what your clients are already doing — and what Taylor can learn from them”

The difference between the 70% of change programs that fail and the 30% that succeed

“Understand before we build”

Transformation Roadmap + AI Vision Document + Architecture Blueprint

ACTIVATE

“Unlock what you already own”

  • ClickUp Brain: from paid-and-forgotten tool to operational intelligence engine
  • Gemini: from “we have it” to email triage, meeting summaries, document drafting

Pillar 3

CONNECT

“One company, one system”

  • Sales ↔ Production visibility (the missing bridge)

Taylor’s people are what make Taylor great. The transformation amplifies them, it does not replace them.

We don’t just fix today. We prepare Taylor to compete in 2028.
