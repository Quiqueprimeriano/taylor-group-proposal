Section 01

Executive Summary

SME Group's franchise portfolio has reached a scale where manual coordination and spreadsheet-driven processes introduce measurable risk to compliance management and financial controls. TransformAZ proposes a two-phase engagement to bring structure, automation, and reliability to the Company's highest-impact workflows:

  • Phase 1 — Discovery & Roadmap (2 weeks, £3,000): diagnostic assessment of current systems, processes, and data flows across two priority areas
  • Phase 2 — Transformation Execution (4 weeks, £6,000): implementation of prioritised improvements validated in Phase 1
  • Total investment: £9,000 over approximately 6 weeks, with Phase 2 scope confirmed only after Phase 1 review

Section 03

Understanding Your Business

SME Group operates a franchise-based portfolio in which individual brands function as standalone legal and operational entities under a centralised head office structure. While franchisor systems govern core in-store technology, the Company retains full responsibility for back-office operations, compliance oversight, financial controls, and cross-brand coordination.

As the portfolio has grown, several core operational workflows have become increasingly reliant on manual coordination, spreadsheet-based tracking, and individual judgement under high volume. These conditions introduce operational risk, reduce consistency across brands, and place unsustainable cognitive load on key personnel.

Two initial Areas of Transformation have been identified as immediate priorities due to their volume, complexity, and sensitivity to error:

Compliance & Repairs / Maintenance

High daily intake of compliance issues and maintenance requests across physical assets demands accurate prioritisation, contractor coordination, and scheduling under tight deadlines. Currently, manual decision-making under pressure increases the risk of missed regulatory deadlines, misallocation of contractor resources, and disruption to store operations — with potential knock-on effects to brand compliance standing.

Income Reconciliation & Financial Controls

Reconciliation of sales, cash, card, and third-party aggregator income across multiple data sources and brands is performed manually, creating inefficiency, delays in financial reporting, and dependency on individual operators. As transaction volume grows, the current process cannot scale without proportional headcount increases — making it a clear candidate for structured automation.

Section 04

Our Approach

The engagement applies a technical and operational diagnostic approach to evaluate how current systems, processes, and data support day-to-day operations and strategic objectives. Rather than attempting broad transformation, we focus on the workflows that create the most operational friction — and bring structure, clarity, and system-level support where it matters most.

Our methodology is built around three core dimensions:

  1. Technology Infrastructure How platforms, tools, and integrations currently operate — and where gaps, redundancies, or underutilised capabilities exist.
  2. Process Design How workflows, handoffs, and manual touchpoints are structured — identifying bottlenecks, dependencies, and areas for standardisation.
  3. Decision Flow How information reaches decision-makers — and whether reporting structures and escalation paths support timely, informed action.

"We don't introduce complexity to manage complexity. We find the structure that was always needed — and build it to last."

TransformAZ Methodology

Key Activities

  • Process mapping to document dependencies, manual touchpoints, and improvement areas
  • Review of existing technology stack, integrations, and data flow across core functions
  • Assessment of system configuration, automation capabilities, and reporting accuracy
  • Stakeholder sessions to capture operational pain points and alignment gaps
  • Synthesis of findings into structured documentation outlining priorities, opportunities, and next steps
Section 05

Engagement Structure

The engagement is structured in two sequential phases that build on each other, designed to enable decisive execution while minimising operational risk and establishing a strong foundation for long-term improvement.

Phase 1 — Discovery & Roadmap

Phase 1 establishes the foundation for the transformation effort by building a deep understanding of the Company's current operational and technological environment. The objective is to identify key Areas of Transformation and determine where improvements in systems, processes, and data structure will create the most impact.

Scope & Activities
  • Conduct an initial review of available documentation, system access, and existing reporting structures
  • Hold stakeholder sessions to understand workflows, dependencies, and operational priorities across both transformation areas
  • Evaluate how key systems are currently used, configured, and interconnected
  • Map high-level processes and data flows to highlight manual steps, redundancies, and integration gaps
Deliverables
  • Current-State Assessment Report — documented overview of systems, processes, and dependencies across both Areas of Transformation
  • Data Flow & Friction Map — visual mapping of data flow, reporting visibility, and operational friction points
  • Transformation Roadmap — prioritised recommendations with defined scope, sequencing, and expected impact for Phase 2
Duration2 weeks at approximately 10 hours/week
Total Hours~20 hours
Rate£150/hr
Investment£3,000 GBP

Phase 2 — Transformation Execution

Phase 2 translates the agreed roadmap into concrete operational improvements. This phase focuses on implementing the prioritised solutions identified during Phase 1, with the objective of reducing manual effort, improving prioritisation and coordination, and increasing operational reliability within the selected workflows.

Scope, sequencing, and pace are defined at the conclusion of Phase 1 to ensure that work is focused on the areas of highest impact and aligned with the Company's operational constraints and risk profile.

Scope & Activities
  • Detailed design of execution workflows, including prioritisation logic, escalation paths, and ownership
  • Configuration and implementation of supporting systems, automation, or decision-support mechanisms
  • Integration of existing data sources and tools where feasible, without disrupting franchisor-mandated systems
  • Definition of operating procedures, controls, and oversight mechanisms to support ongoing use
  • Iterative validation with stakeholders to ensure solutions function as intended in practice
Deliverables
  • Configured Workflows & Automation — implemented prioritisation logic, routing rules, and automation for target processes
  • Operating Procedures Document — clear ownership, escalation logic, and step-by-step procedures for the new workflows
  • Integration & Data Mapping — connected data sources with validated data flow and reconciliation checks
  • Stakeholder Validation Report — documented results of user acceptance testing and iterative adjustments
  • Scalability Playbook — practical foundation and guidelines for extending improvements to additional areas

Expected Outcomes & Success Criteria

The following framework outlines the key performance indicators that will be used to measure the impact of the transformation effort. These metrics will be validated and baselined during Phase 1 and tracked throughout Phase 2 execution.

Compliance & Repairs / Maintenance

KPI What We'll Measure
Response Time Reduction in average time from issue intake to contractor assignment
Resolution Rate Increase in issues resolved within SLA or regulatory deadline
Error Reduction Decrease in misrouted, duplicated, or missed compliance items
Prioritisation Accuracy Consistency of priority assignment vs. actual urgency and regulatory impact

Income Reconciliation & Financial Controls

KPI What We'll Measure
Reconciliation Time Reduction in hours spent on manual reconciliation per reporting cycle
Accuracy Rate Improvement in first-pass reconciliation accuracy across income sources
Operator Dependency Reduction in reliance on specific individuals to complete reconciliation
Reporting Latency Faster availability of reconciled financial data to management

These KPIs are presented as a measurement framework to be validated during Phase 1. Specific baseline values and targets will be established through the Discovery process, based on actual current-state data and stakeholder input.
